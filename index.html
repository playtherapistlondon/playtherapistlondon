About Me

I am a fully qualified Therapeutic Play Practitioner. FAQs – Frequently Asked Questions

Who is Play Therapy suitable for?

Play Therapy is suitable for children aged 3-11 who may be experiencing emotional, behavioural, or social challenges. This includes children dealing with anxiety, trauma, loss, attachment difficulties, or difficulties in peer relationships.

How many sessions are typically required?

Each child’s therapeutic needs are unique. While some children may benefit from short-term interventions (e.g. 12 sessions), others may require longer-term support. The duration of therapy is regularly reviewed and discussed with parents or carers.

Do parents participate in the sessions?

In non-directive play therapy, sessions are held individually with the child. However, parental involvement is a crucial part of the overall process. Regular parent review meetings are arranged to discuss the child’s progress and offer support for the family.

How are sessions planned?

Sessions follow a non-directive, child-led approach. The therapy room is equipped with a variety of therapeutic play materials, including art, clay, a sand tray, role-play, puppets, and storytelling tools, allowing the child to express themselves freely and safely.

Is everything my child shares confidential?

Yes. All information shared during sessions is treated with strict confidentiality. However, if any safeguarding concerns arise regarding the child’s safety or wellbeing, relevant information may be shared in line with safeguarding policies and legal obligations.
